Famous Temples in Yogyakarta

Borobudur Temple

Borobudur Temple is the world’s largest Buddhist monument, renowned for its intricate stone carvings and majestic stupas, symbolizing the path to enlightenment amid lush Central Javanese landscapes.

IDR 455.000 (Foreigners)

Prambanan Temple

The largest Hindu temple in Indonesia, dedicated to the Trimurti: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

IDR 50.000

Sewu Temple

Located within the Prambanan complex, this stunning Buddhist temple features hundreds of smaller accompanying shrines.

IDR 50.000

Plaosan Temple

A beautiful Buddhist temple with mixed Hindu-Buddhist architecture, often used as a backdrop for pre-wedding photo sessions.

IDR 50.000

Ratu Boko Temple

An archaeological site of a former royal palace complex, offering breathtaking sunset views from its hilltop location.

IDR 85.000

Sambisari Temple

A Hindu temple that was once buried by volcanic ash, now an intriguing site due to its unique position below ground level.

IDR 50.000

Kalasan Temple

The oldest Buddhist temple in Yogyakarta, built in the 8th century to honor the goddess Tara.

IDR 50.000

Sari Temple

A former Buddhist monastery temple where monks once lived, located close to Kalasan Temple.

IDR 50.000

Must Visit Destinations

Malioboro Street

A lively street at the heart of Yogyakarta filled with street vendors, souvenir shops, and local food stalls, perfect for cultural strolls and shopping.

Free

Taman Sari Water Castle

Once a royal garden of the Sultan, this charming historical site features pools, hidden tunnels, and elegant ruins steeped in Javanese history.

Free

Keraton Yogyakarta (The Palace)

A grand symbol of Javanese culture and governance, the palace is still the home of the Sultan and hosts daily cultural performances.

Free

Parangtritis Beach

Known for its mystical aura and stunning sunsets, this beach combines natural beauty with traditional beliefs and local legends.

IDR 10.000 + Parking Fee

Mount Merapi Lava Tour

Ride a jeep across volcanic trails and abandoned villages while learning about the impact of Merapi’s eruptions and local resilience.

IDR 350.000 / Jeep trip

Bukit Bintang

A scenic hilltop spot offering romantic night views of the city lights, popular with both tourists and locals.

IDR 8.000 / Person

Kalibiru National Park

A peaceful nature retreat with panoramic views, zip lines, and photo platforms overlooking the Menoreh Hills and Sermo Reservoir.

IDR 15.000

Goa Jomblang

A vertical cave with a heavenly light beam that filters through the roof, ideal for adventurous caving and unique photo ops.

IDR 400.000 / Person

Pindul Cave Tubing

Enjoy a relaxing yet thrilling ride on an inner tube along an underground river through ancient limestone formations.

IDR 300.000 / Person

Alun-Alun Kidul (Southern Square)

A popular public space for family fun, famous for trying to walk blindfolded between twin banyan trees at night.

Free

Food & Beverages in Yogyakarta

Gudeg

The city’s signature jackfruit stew, usually served with chicken, egg, and coconut rice.

Start Form 7.000

Bakpia Pathok

Small sweet pastries filled with mung bean or variants like chocolate, cheese, and durian—classic Yogyakarta souvenirs.

Start From 15.000

Sate Klathak

Goat meat skewers seasoned simply and grilled on bicycle spokes, served with rich gule sauce.

Start From 12.000

Oseng-Oseng Mercon

Spicy beef stir-fry literally named “firecracker” for its explosive heat.

Start From 10.000

Bakmi Jawa

Savory fried or soupy noodles topped with chicken and spring onions.

Start From 8.000

Mie Ayam Grabyas

Chicken noodle soup topped with crispy chicken skin bits—served late-night on Malioboro.

Start From 13.000

Soto Lenthok

Chicken soup served with cassava fritters—a comforting local dish.

Start From 12.000

Angkringan Delights: Kopi Joss & Sego Kucing

Charcoal-cooled coffee and tiny rice packs—staples of Jogja's street-food scene.

Start From 5.000

Es Buah / Dawet

Chilled fruit cocktails or traditional rice-jelly dessert—perfect to beat the heat.

Start From 5.000

Lumpia Jawa (Yogya Spring Rolls)

Crispy spring rolls filled with chicken, vegetables, and often quail egg—served with pickles and garlic.

Start From 5.000

Brongkos

A royal stew from Keraton culture with meat, beans, spices, and egg in coconut milk.

Start From 15.000

Mie Lethek & Nasi Bogana

Regional specialties-cassava noodles and banana-leaf-wrapped mixed rice, both hearty and traditional.

Start From 12.000
